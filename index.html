about us

CENTRAL zoo is one of the biggest zoo in the town. The land area of the zoo is 33 acres. CENTRAL zoo was established in the year 1980 with 10 acre land but lately it was modified and land area was also added in the year 2000. Proper residing space for all the type of animals has been made and maintains human security at high rate. Proper supply of food for all the animals is maintained and checked at regular intervals.

FAQs

Why are the monkeys` rear ends swollen?
Lincoln Park Zoo veterinarian Meehan responds that in the Lincoln Park chimpanzees and Brookfield mandrills and Guinea baboons, a swollen rear end is normal. ''Evolution has provided these primarily ground-dwelling apes and monkeys with their own seat pads, called the ischial callosities,'' Meehan says.
Are those snakes real?
In the 1970s, some not-so-reputable zoos duped the public by sneaking acrylic snakes into exhibits. Maraldi says that some people remember those stories and are cynical, others just expect to snakes to bound around their enclosures like many warm-blooded animals. But for the often lethargic, cold- blooded snakes, spending hours at a time motionless is perfectly normal behavior.
Where do you turn in, report or find a lost child?
At Brookfield Zoo, it`s at the First Aid and Security Office located at the South Gate. At Lincoln Park Zoo, the lost people can be retrieved or reported at the information desk at the east side of the Kovler Lion House.
What does the zoo do with excess animals?
Brewer says that responsible zoos using sophisticated management techniques are able to prevent an excess by administering various kinds of birth control. Females might be implanted with a capsule which will prevent contraception. Examples of Brookfield residents on birth control include the mandrill, spectacled bear, orangutan, siamang and capuchin monkey.
